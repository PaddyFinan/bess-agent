## 4. POLICY & REGULATORY DEVELOPMENTS

### 🔴 MATERIAL UPDATE: ITALIAN ELECTRICITY MARKET REFORM

**Event:** Italy's cabinet approved "Energy Decree" (€3bn+ package) stripping carbon costs from retail power bills, with government reimbursing gas-fired generators for EU ETS permit costs.

**Date:** Mid-February 2026 (confirmed implementation progressing)

**Impact Assessment:**

**Potential Concern:** If carbon costs are removed from wholesale market price formation (not just retail bills), this could **compress evening peak prices** as CCGTs would bid lower absent carbon pass-through, directly reducing battery arbitrage spreads.

**Mitigating Factors:**
- Decree appears focused on **retail bill relief** with government reimbursement to generators — suggesting wholesale price formation may be unaffected
- If generators receive carbon cost reimbursement but still face ETS compliance costs, they would continue bidding carbon costs into wholesale market
- TTF gas price trends (+9.3%) currently **offsetting any theoretical carbon-related compression**

**Action Required:** 🟡 **CLARIFICATION NEEDED** — Request legal/regulatory opinion on whether Energy Decree affects wholesale electricity price formation or only retail tariff structure. If wholesale pricing mechanism unchanged, no revenue impact. If carbon removed from wholesale bids, **re-underwriting required**.
