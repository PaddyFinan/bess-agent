## 2. POLICY & REGULATORY NEWS: **BEARISH** ⚠️

### MACSE Auction Results — **MATERIAL RISK TO LONG-TERM MERCHANT ECONOMICS**

**First Auction Results (October 2025):**
- All 10 GWh awarded at **€12,959/MWh/year** — **65% below reserve price** (€37,000/MWh/year)
- Enel captured >50% of capacity; largest project: 3.6 GWh in Southern Italy
- **Second auction announced (March 2026)** — further capacity to be auctioned

**Implications for This Project:**
1. **MACSE creates subsidised competition** — contracted projects can undercut merchant revenues in day-ahead/intraday markets by accepting lower margins
2. **Merchant revenue compression risk** — if MACSE projects flood the market, arbitrage opportunities may deteriorate as more storage competes for the same spreads
3. **Financing preference shift** — lenders may increasingly require MACSE contracts for new projects, reducing appetite for pure merchant structures

**Re-underwriting trigger?** **NOT YET**, but monitor closely:
- Current project remains competitive (Northern Italy, already operational, no MACSE contract dependency)
- Risk is **medium-term** (2027+) as MACSE projects commission
- **Action required if:** merchant spreads compress >15% over next 6 months without corresponding gas price decline
