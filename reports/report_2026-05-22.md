## **5. POLICY & REGULATORY WATCH**

### **🚨 FLAG: MACSE AUCTION RESULT — BEARISH FOR MERCHANT STRUCTURES**

**Issue:** Italy's first MACSE capacity auction (Oct 2025) cleared at **€12,959/MWh-year**, ~65% below €37,000 reserve price and ~40% below market expectations (€21k–22k range). Oversubscription was 4x.

**Impact on this project:**
- **Current structure:** 100% merchant arbitrage + €1.5M/year fixed ancillary services
- **Risk:** If lenders or off-takers adopt €13k/MWh-year as the new benchmark for capacity value, this could:
  - Compress future ancillary service revenue assumptions
  - Reduce bankability of pure merchant structures
  - Shift lender preference toward MACSE-contracted or hybrid projects

**Mitigating factors:**
- This project's revenue model already assumes **€1.5M/year ancillary (~€60k/MW)**, which is conservative vs. MACSE clearing (~€13k/MWh-year × 50 MWh = €650k/year)
- **Current DSCR headroom:** Base case at 2.08x leaves significant cushion even if ancillary revenues compress by 50%

**Action required:** No immediate re-underwriting necessary, but recommend:
1. Stress-test revenue model with ancillary services at €13k/MWh-year (€650k/year) instead of €1.5M
2. Monitor whether private capacity contracts (e.g., Terna) adopt MACSE pricing as reference
3. Evaluate contractual optionality: could project pivot to MACSE participation in future auctions?
