## REGULATORY & POLICY UPDATE

### Key Developments
1. **Second MACSE Auction (2029 delivery):** Italy's regulator ARERA has approved targeting 16 GWh of storage capacity for the next auction. This confirms sustained government commitment to long-term contracted storage buildout beyond the first 10 GWh auction (October 2025).

2. **MACSE Pricing Shock:** Market intelligence indicates the first auction cleared at **€12,959/MWh/year** vs. €37,000 cap — a 65% discount that has created downward pressure on ready-to-build BESS asset valuations.

3. **Merchant Market Sentiment:** Reports suggest "regulatory uncertainty" following MACSE results, though this primarily affects projects seeking CfD-style contracts rather than pure merchant projects like ours.

### Impact Assessment
**NO CHANGE TO REVENUE ASSUMPTIONS REQUIRED.**

Our project is a pure merchant play relying on day-ahead arbitrage and ancillary services (€1.5M/year fixed), not MACSE contracts. The low MACSE clearing price reflects overcapacity in the contracted segment but does not directly affect merchant arbitrage spreads, which are driven by:
- Intraday power price volatility (currently strong at €134.66/MWh average spread)
- Gas-fired generation setting marginal prices (bullish with TTF rising)
- Ancillary services demand (Terna grid needs independent of MACSE)

**However:** Monitor whether MACSE-driven oversupply in 2029 onwards begins to compress merchant spreads through increased competition. Not an immediate concern but flagged for 2027-2028 forward curve analysis.
